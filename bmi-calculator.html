Under Weight

Eat more often: Eat smaller meals and snacks throughout the day, instead of three large meals. This can help you feel more full and boost your appetite.
Add calories: Add healthy calories to your meals by adding cheese, nuts, seeds, or full fat dairy. You can also add dry or liquid milk to foods like mashed potatoes or soups.
Choose full fat foods: Select full fat foods instead of low or non-fat options.
Exercise regularly: Exercise can help you build muscle and stimulate your appetite.

Normal Weight

Eat healthy foods: Eat a variety of fruits and vegetables, and choose foods that are low in sugar and fat. You can also try to replace fatty meats with lean meats, beans, lentils, or fish.
Be physically active: Aim for at least 150 minutes of physical activity per week.
Avoid mindless eating: Try not to eat while watching TV, as this can lead to eating more than you need.

Over Weight

Create a treatment plan: Work with a healthcare provider to develop a treatment plan that includes lifestyle changes, diet, exercise, and other strategies.
Eat healthy foods Eat a balanced diet that's calorie-controlled, and follow the Dietary Guidelines for Americans.
Exercise regularly Try to get at least 30 minutes of moderate-intensity physical activity most days of the week. You can try walking, jogging, swimming, or tennis.
Drink water Water is calorie-free and can help you feel full, which can prevent overeating.
